## Anti-Patterns Verdict
**LLM:** Not obviously AI-made — the chrome/steel metallic system, the real minted coins, the freight-relay POV, and the verbatim driver voice give it a genuine identity. Two residual AI-grammar tells remain: (1) a small uppercase mono **eyebrow above nearly every one of ~13 sections**, and (2) several **near-identical card grids** stacked across a long page.
**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` over the drivers markup (AudiencePage, page, HowItWorks, Bits, OneHomeCalculator) = **clean, 0 findings**. The known gradient-text utility (`text-*-fill`) lives in globals.css and is used on the homepage, **not** on /drivers. Agreement: detector clean; the eyebrow cadence + length are judgment tells it can't catch.

## Overall Impression
A confident, distinctive driver page with a strong narrative arc (club story → free membership → services → fleet → space → webinar → tour, then the OneHome value + proof, then the form) and clean black↔white rhythm. Biggest opportunity: it reads slightly "templated" because almost every section wears a tiny kicker and several sections are the same card-grid shape. Thin the eyebrows and vary a couple of the grids and it stops looking AI-assembled.

## What's Working
1. **Narrative IA.** The join cluster leads, the OneHome value/proof follows, the form closes. Reading order is intentional and earns the scroll.
2. **Distinctive brand material.** Minted OneHome/Outriders coins, chrome/steel frames, the dual-metal Jeff lockup, and the script "Welcome to the Club" are specific and non-generic.
3. **Voice + CTA discipline.** Verbatim driver copy in the Jeff register; CTAs limited to Join Free / Connect / Schedule, repeated at the right moments.

## Priority Issues
- **[P1] Eyebrow on (nearly) every section.** ~13 sections each carry a small uppercase mono kicker ("The Outriders", "Free membership", "Three ways to get Space", "Monthly webinars", "Take the Tour", problem/how/memphis/form kickers…). This is the single clearest "AI made this" tell on the page and is an explicit brand-register ban. Bits.tsx even documents the intent as "landmarks, never an eyebrow stamped above every section" — the page drifted from that. *Fix:* keep kickers on 3-4 landmark sections, drop them elsewhere; let the headline + content carry the rest. *Command:* `$impeccable typeset` (or `quieter`).
- **[P2] Length + repeated card-grid shape.** Steps (3 cards), Services (12 cells), Fleet (6 cards), Space (3 cards), plus memphis stat cards and the gallery — a lot of same-rhythm grids on a 14-section page. Risks card-grid fatigue and pushes the savings calculator far down. *Fix:* vary at least one grid (e.g., Services as a two-column definition list, not cards), and consider trimming the 12-item Services menu or collapsing Services+Fleet visual treatment so they don't read as the same module twice. *Command:* `$impeccable distill` / `layout`.
- **[P2] "SPACE" headline is ambiguous on first read.** The giant one-word "SPACE" headline assumes the reader already maps "Space" → terminal/parking access. A first-time driver may not. *Fix:* pair the word with a one-line clarifier in the headline lockup or make the eyebrow do more work ("Three ways to get terminal Space"). *Command:* `$impeccable clarify`.
- **[P3] Metallic-accent inconsistency.** Star flourishes are chrome on the ink Services band but accent-orange on the white Fleet band; three metallic coins (OneHome in hero, Outriders in story + Services crest) plus two CSS numeral-coin systems (steps, ways). Reads slightly busy. *Fix:* one star color rule, and cap the hero/story/services to a single Outriders coin moment. *Command:* `$impeccable polish`.

## Persona Red Flags
**Jordan (first-time driver):** Lands well — story-first is reassuring. But the lone **"SPACE"** headline is jargon-shaped without inline context; Jordan may not connect it to parking/terminal access. The 14-section scroll also delays the concrete "what do I get / what's it cost" (calculator) until well below the fold.
**Casey (mobile, one-handed):** Hero "Join Free" sits at the top (out of the thumb zone) and the next Join Free is deep in the Space band; a sticky/bottom CTA would help one-handed signup. Long scroll on mobile; the savings calculator is far down. Vimeo is correctly responsive 16:9 and lazy.
**Riley (stress-tester):** Headlines hold at 390/768/1440 with 0 overflow; Vimeo shows its own thumbnail (no branded poster yet — flagged TODO). No broken states found. The one-word "SPACE" at a 120px clamp is the riskiest line but still fits.

## Minor Observations
- Body font is **Newsreader**, which is on the register's reflex-reject list — but it's a committed part of the LHS brand system, so identity-preservation wins. Note, not a defect.
- Numbered markers (Step #1–3, item 01–12) are fine where they're real sequences (the 3-step membership); the 01–12 on the Services menu is decorative numbering on a non-sequence.
- Removing the photo mosaic (good de-dup) means the club is now described in text (Services) and shown in photos only in the gallery; the gallery is carrying all the club imagery alone.
- Hero scroll cue now targets `#welcome` (good — no longer skips the new cluster).

## Questions to Consider
- If every section lost its kicker except three, which three would you keep?
- Does the driver need the full 12-item Services menu, or would 6 hero amenities + "and more" land harder?
- Should the savings calculator move back up (it's the sharpest hook for a driver) or stay below the club story?
